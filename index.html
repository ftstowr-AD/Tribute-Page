A Tribute to Satyajit Ray

Born : 2 May 1921 - Died : 23 April 1992

Biography

Satyajit Ray was one of India’s greatest filmmakers, known worldwide for his realistic and artistic cinema.
He was born on May 2, 1921, in Kolkata, into a culturally rich Bengali family. His father, Sukumar Ray, was
a famous writer and illustrator. Ray studied at Presidency College and later at Visva-Bharati University,
where he developed a deep interest in art and culture. Before becoming a filmmaker, he worked as a
graphic designer and illustrator. His first film, Pather Panchali (1955), brought him international fame.
It was the first part of the famous Apu Trilogy, followed by Aparajito and Apur Sansar. His films focused on
human emotions, everyday life, and social issues, making them relatable and timeless. Over his career, Ray
directed more than 30 films. Some of his notable works include Charulata, The Music Room, and Goopy
Gyne Bagha Byne. He was also a writer and created popular fictional characters like Feluda and Professor
Shonku. Ray received many prestigious awards, including the Academy Honorary Award (Oscar) in 1992 for
his lifetime achievement in cinema. He also received India’s highest civilian award, the Bharat Ratna. He
passed away on April 23, 1992, but his legacy continues to inspire filmmakers and artists around the world.

Key Achievements

  • Apu Trilogy Success - His films Pather Panchali, Aparajito, and Apur Sansar became
    world-famous and changed Indian cinema.

  • Academy Honorary Award (Oscar) - He received a lifetime achievement Oscar from
    the Academy Awards.

  • 🇮🇳 Bharat Ratna - India’s highest civilian award for his contribution to art and cinema.

  • Padma Awards - He was honored with Padma Bhushan and Padma Vibhushan.

  • Famous Writer - Created iconic characters like Feluda and Professor Shonku.
